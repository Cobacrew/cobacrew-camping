A regular bath towel is one of the worst things you can pack for camping — it's bulky, it takes forever to dry, and a damp cotton towel in a stuffed pack is a recipe for mildew smell by day two. Rainleaf's microfiber towel solves all three problems, which is why it's sitting at over 30,000 ratings and an Amazon's Choice badge in the camping category.

![Hand holding a gray microfiber towel](https://images.pexels.com/photos/11370616/pexels-photo-11370616.jpeg?auto=compress&cs=tinysrgb&w=1200)

## Why microfiber beats cotton for camping specifically

- **Packs down small.** It compresses into its included pouch to roughly the size of a fist — a cotton towel doesn't come close.
- **Dries fast.** Microfiber's fine weave holds far less water and air-dries in a fraction of the time a cotton towel needs, which matters when you're packing it away wet the next morning.
- **Actually absorbent.** Despite being thin, quality microfiber towels absorb several times their weight in water — this one holds up well against that claim in practice.
- **Anti-microbial treatment.** Reduces the mildew smell that ruins packed-away cotton towels on multi-day trips.

## Where it's genuinely the right call

- **Backpacking, where every ounce and cubic inch counts.**
- **Multi-day trips**, where a towel needs to dry between uses rather than staying damp in a stuff sack.
- **Travel beyond camping** — it doubles well as a gym, pool, or travel towel, which is part of why it sells as well as it does.

## Where a regular towel still wins

If you're car camping with unlimited trunk space and don't care about pack weight, a plush cotton towel is simply more comfortable against skin. Microfiber has a slightly different, thinner feel that some people need to get used to. This is a genuine tradeoff, not a flaw — you're trading a small comfort difference for a large packability and dry-time advantage.
