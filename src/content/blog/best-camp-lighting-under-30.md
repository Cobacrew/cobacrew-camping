Lighting is cheap, but it's easy to either underbuy (fumbling with a phone flashlight while cooking) or overbuy (five different lanterns you don't need). Here's the setup that covers a first trip for well under $30 per person.

## The one non-negotiable: a headlamp per person

A headlamp is the single highest-value camping purchase under $15. Hands-free light while cooking, setting up a tent in the dark, or walking to the bathroom at night matters more than almost any other gear decision. Look for:

- **At least 150 lumens** — enough to actually see the trail, not just enough to see your feet
- **A red-light mode**, which preserves night vision and doesn't blind everyone else at the picnic table
- **Adjustable brightness**, so you're not burning through batteries on full power for a task that needs a dim glow

## One lantern for the site

A single lantern hung from the tent ceiling or a tree branch covers your main gathering space. LED lantern options under $20 easily run 8+ hours on a set of batteries. Look for:

- **A hanging hook or handle**
- **At least a couple of brightness settings** — full power for setup and cleanup, low power for sitting around after dark

## What to skip on a first trip

- **String lights.** They look nice in photos but add cost and a fussy setup for something a single lantern already handles.
- **Multiple lanterns.** One per gathering zone is enough; a second is a "nice to have," not a first-trip essential.
- **Expensive rechargeable systems.** Battery-powered lights are simpler and cheaper to start with — rechargeable setups make more sense once you're camping often enough to justify the investment.

## Battery strategy

Bring one full spare set of batteries for your headlamp and lantern combined, not per device. Running your headlamp on low power for routine tasks and saving full brightness for when you actually need it will make a set of batteries last an entire weekend trip in most cases.

## Bottom line

A $12–15 headlamp per person plus one $15–20 lantern for the site covers a first trip completely. Everything past that is a comfort upgrade, not a requirement.
