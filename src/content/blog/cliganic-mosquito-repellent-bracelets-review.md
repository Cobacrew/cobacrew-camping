Repellent bracelets get a mixed reputation, and honestly, some of that skepticism is fair — they're not a replacement for spray on a real camping trip. But they've earned a permanent spot as an Amazon Camping & Hiking bestseller because there's a specific, narrow use case where they're genuinely the better tool. Here's an honest look at Cliganic's version.

## How they actually work

Cliganic's bracelets are infused with natural plant oils (typically citronella, lemongrass, and similar botanicals) rather than DEET or Picaridin. They work by releasing scent into the immediate air around your wrist — not by creating a protective zone around your whole body. That distinction matters a lot for setting the right expectations.

## What they're genuinely good for

- **Low-effort, low-commitment protection** — no spraying, no greasy skin, just clip it on.
- **Kids.** No spray means no worrying about a toddler rubbing repellent into their eyes, and the natural-oil formula is a common preference for families avoiding synthetic repellents on young kids.
- **Short exposure** — a backyard evening, a quick walk, sitting around a low-mosquito campsite.
- **A backup layer.** Wearing one alongside spray on ankles/other exposed skin covers the gap a bracelet leaves on its own.

## Where they fall short

Be clear-eyed about the limits: bracelets protect a small radius around the wrist, not your whole body, and mosquitoes will happily bite ankles, necks, and any exposed skin outside that radius. In heavy mosquito conditions — think dusk near standing water — a bracelet alone is not enough. This isn't a Cliganic-specific weakness; it's true of every bracelet-format repellent regardless of brand.

## What buyers say

Reviews consistently mention the same two things: they're a nice light-touch option for kids and casual use, and buyers who expected full-body protection from a wrist bracelet alone were disappointed — which comes back to setting expectations correctly rather than a product flaw. The individually wrapped 10-pack format also gets praise for keeping unused bracelets fresh and making it easy to hand one to each person in a group.

## Bottom line

Cliganic's bracelets do exactly what a repellent bracelet can do — light, no-spray protection in a small radius, ideal for kids and short outdoor stretches. They're not a substitute for spray on a real camping trip in buggy conditions, but as a backup layer or a low-commitment option for light exposure, they're a smart $8 addition to a camping bag.
